AreaChart Visualization

Purpose

This areachart visualization is to view the trends of total passengers flying within a year from SFO Airport from 2005 to 2019 (as of February 2020).

Wrangling

For this areachart the data was filtered using Activity Period and Passenger Count from Data SF.
The used .csv file can be found here.

Tableau

Data set was taken from Data SF and shows the Air Traffic Passenger Statistics for the San Francisco international airport.
Data period from July 2005 to September 2019 (as of this date in February 2020)

Analysis

Passenger count is encoded as the y-axis of chart thus higher peeks represent higher passenger count.
Months are represented as the x-axis, so total passengers from 2005-2019 time period can be observed in a monthly viewpoint.

From this linechart we can see that flights increase from the February-August in a steady rate while having a decrease within September-January. Information that can be extrapulated from this data. First, there is a large flow of passengers at the start of the years while a decrease at the end of the years. This is surprisingly interesting because most of the capitalistic holidays are near the end of a year (thanksgiving, christmas, new years). Additionally, this color coded chart allows a visual representation of passengers to Terminals. An notible visual is that Terminal 2 is often the least traveled termial while Termials 1, 3, and International are most frequented when flying through SFO.
